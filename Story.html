I

Bala Kanda

The Book of Youth · 77 Sargas · Chapters of Origin

The universe breathes. In the sacred city of Ayodhya on the banks of the Sarayu, King Dasharatha of the Ikshvaku dynasty prays for an heir. The heavens answer — and so begins an eternal story.

Book One

Opening Shloka — Valmiki Ramayana 1.1.1

तपः स्वाध्यायनिरतं तपस्वी वाग्विदां वरम् ।
नारदं परिपप्रच्छ वाल्मीकिर्मुनिपुङ्गवम् ॥

Valmiki, the foremost of sages, deep in austerity and Vedic learning, approached the divine sage Narada — best among those who wield words — and asked him his eternal question.

Balakanda · Sarga 1 · Shloka 1

Sarga 1–4 🪷

The Question That Sparked an Epic

Sage Valmiki asks Narada: who in this world is the most virtuous? Narada's answer births the Ramayana.

Origin
Sarga 14–16 🔥

Putrakameshti — The Sacred Fire

A great yajna is performed. From the sacrificial fire, Vishnu descends as four divine sons into the royal household of Ayodhya.

Divine Birth
Sarga 17–30 ⚔️

Vishwamitra's Forest — First Battle

Young Rama accompanies sage Vishwamitra into the forest, defeats Tataka, and receives divine celestial weapons.

Coming of Age
Sarga 65–68 🏹

The Breaking of Shiva's Bow

At Mithila's swayamvara, Rama lifts and breaks Shiva's mighty bow — and wins the hand of Sita Devi.

Sacred Union
Sarga 71–77 🌸

Divine Weddings & Parashurama

Four royal weddings unite Ayodhya and Mithila. Parashurama arrives in fury — and departs in reverence.

Celebration

II

Ayodhya Kanda

The Book of Ayodhya · 119 Sargas · The Great Exile

Joy turns to grief in a single night. On the eve of Rama's coronation, a palace intrigue orchestrated by Queen Kaikeyi shatters a kingdom — and tests the mettle of every soul within it.

Book Two

Kaikeyi's Boon — The Fateful Words

रामं दशरथो राजा वनवासाय राघवम् ।
प्रेषयामास धर्मात्मा कैकेय्या वचनं श्रुत्वा ॥

Dasharatha, the righteous king, hearing the words of Kaikeyi, sent Rama — descendant of Raghu — into exile in the forest.

Ayodhyakanda · Sarga 18

Sarga 1–15 🪔

The Eve of Coronation

Ayodhya blazes with joy as Rama's coronation is announced. But in the palace, Manthara plants a poisonous seed in Kaikeyi's heart.

The Turn
Sarga 9–16 💔

Kaikeyi's Two Boons

Kaikeyi invokes two ancient boons: Bharata's coronation and Rama's fourteen-year exile. Dasharatha is shattered.

Tragedy
Sarga 16–25 🌿

Rama's Serene Acceptance

Rama accepts exile without anger. Sita and Lakshmana refuse to be left behind. Three royal souls walk into the forest.

Dharma
Sarga 63–65 🕯️

The Passing of Dasharatha

Separated from Rama, Dasharatha dies of grief — fulfilling an ancient curse. Ayodhya mourns in darkness.

Loss
Sarga 99–116 👣

Bharata and the Golden Padukas

Bharata refuses the throne, places Rama's sandals upon it, and rules Ayodhya as a devoted regent for fourteen years.

Devotion

III

Aranya Kanda

The Book of the Forest · 75 Sargas · The Abduction

In the dark forests of Dandaka, Rama dispenses justice to demons and meets ascetics. But fate darkens — Ravana plots, a golden deer deceives, and the greatest calamity of the epic unfolds.

Book Three

Sita's Abduction — The Turning Point

रावणः सीतामुत्क्षिप्य ययौ सहसा तया ।
रोदन्तीं राघवेति च लक्ष्मणेति च वादिनीम् ॥

Ravana seized Sita and departed swiftly with her, as she cried out — "O Raghava! O Lakshmana!"

Aranyakanda · Sarga 49

Sarga 17–19 👁️

Surpanakha's Wrath

Demoness Surpanakha is humiliated by Lakshmana. Her fury carries her to Lanka — where she describes Sita to Ravana.

Catalyst
Sarga 42–45 🦌

The Golden Deer of Illusion

Maricha becomes a magical golden deer to lure Rama. The cruel stratagem leaves Sita unguarded in the forest.

Deception
Sarga 45–50 🌩️

The Abduction of Sita Devi

Ravana abducts Sita. The noble eagle Jatayu fights valiantly but falls. The darkest hour of the Ramayana begins.

Crisis
Sarga 64–68 🦅

Jatayu's Sacred Sacrifice

Dying Jatayu tells Rama the direction of Sita's abduction. Rama performs the eagle's last rites with the love of a son.

Sacrifice

IV

Kishkindha Kanda

The Book of Kishkindha · 67 Sargas · Alliance of the Vanaras

In the Rishyamukha forest, a chance meeting changes everything. Rama forges an eternal alliance with the Vanara kingdom — and the greatest devotee in history steps forward.

Book Four

Hanuman's First Meeting with Rama

कस्त्वं बाहुबलोपेतः कस्य वा त्वं मनोरमः ।
सन्ध्याभ्रमिव सौम्यात्मा प्रभया ते विराजसे ॥

Who are you, endowed with the strength of arms? Whose are you, O beautiful one? You shine like a twilight cloud, O gentle-souled one, with your radiance.

Kishkindhakanda · Sarga 3 (Hanuman to Rama)

Sarga 1–4 🐒

Rama Meets Hanuman

Hanuman, sent by Sugriva, meets Rama for the first time. The Lord recognises his greatest devotee in the wisest of Vanaras.

Sacred Meeting
Sarga 15–18 ⚡

The Fall of Vali

Rama slays Vali from hiding — a much-debated act. Vali questions, Rama answers with dharma, and a king accepts his fate.

Justice
Sarga 64–67 🌊

Hanuman Awakens His Power

Jambavan reminds Hanuman of his divine strength. The Vanara god grows enormous and leaps across the ocean toward Lanka.

Awakening

V

Sundara Kanda

The Beautiful Book · 68 Sargas · Hanuman in Lanka

Named 'Beautiful' — for it is the most beloved kanda in tradition. Hanuman's flight to Lanka, his search for Sita, and his blazing return form the emotional and spiritual apex of the Ramayana.

Book Five

Hanuman Finds Sita

सा तं दृष्ट्वा हनुमन्तं सीता परमदुःखिता ।
मनसा चिन्तयामास कः श्रेयस्करो मम ॥

Sita, seeing Hanuman, deeply distressed, thought in her mind: Who could bring me greater welfare than this one?

Sundarakanda · Sarga 32

Sarga 1–4 🌏

The Great Leap

Hanuman leaps across a hundred yojanas of ocean. A journey that marks the single greatest individual feat of devotion in all literature.

Devotion
Sarga 13–22 🌳

Sita in the Ashoka Vatika

Hanuman finds Sita — sorrowful but unbroken, holding a blade of grass between herself and Ravana's demands.

Fortitude
Sarga 30–36 💍

The Ring and the Jewel

Hanuman gives Sita Rama's ring. Sita gives him her chudamani. These two tokens become the proof of faith across an ocean.

Messenger
Sarga 50–55 🔥

Lanka Ablaze

Hanuman's burning tail becomes a torch. He sets the golden city of Lanka on fire — a message of divine fury from Rama.

Fiery Warning

VI

Yuddha Kanda

The Book of War · 128 Sargas · The Battle of Lanka

The longest kanda — the great war itself. Dharma meets adharma on the beaches and ramparts of Lanka. Gods descend to witness. Heaven holds its breath as Rama and Ravana finally face each other.

Book Six

The Death of Ravana

स हत्वा राक्षसान् घोरान् सुग्रीवेण समागतः ।
प्रहृष्टो जग्मतुस्तत्र यत्र रामो व्यवस्थितः ॥

Having slain the terrible Rakshasas and joined with Sugriva, they went joyfully to where Rama stood steady and victorious.

Yuddhakanda · Sarga 108

Sarga 19–24 🌉

The Building of Rama Setu

Rocks inscribed with Rama's name float upon the ocean. The Vanara army builds a miraculous bridge across one hundred yojanas of sea.

Divine Bridge
Sarga 17–19 🕊️

Vibhishana Takes Refuge

Ravana's righteous brother surrenders to Rama, who accepts him and crowns him king of Lanka — before the first battle is fought.

Sharanagati
Sarga 88–91 🌿

Hanuman and the Sanjeevani

Lakshmana falls to Indrajit's divine weapon. Hanuman carries an entire mountain of life-giving herbs to save him.

Sacrifice
Sarga 107–111 ⚡

The Fall of Ravana

Rama's Brahmastra pierces Ravana's navel — the source of his immortality. The demon king falls. Dharma is restored. Heaven rejoices.

Victory of Dharma
Sarga 115–118 🔥

Sita's Agni Pariksha

Sita enters the sacred fire to prove her purity. Agni himself rises bearing her — untouched — before all three worlds.

Sacred Trial

VII

Uttara Kanda

The Final Book · 111 Sargas · Rama's Reign & Return

The story continues beyond the war. Rama's rule — the Rama Rajya — becomes the ideal of governance. But dharma's burden is never lifted from the righteous. The final chapter carries the deepest sorrow, and the most transcendent ending.

Book Seven

Rama Rajya — The Ideal Kingdom

नित्यपुष्पा नित्यफला नित्यच्छाया सुशीतला ।
वाताः सुखस्पर्शा वान्ति पुष्पेभ्यो मधुगन्धिनः ॥

Trees forever in flower, forever bearing fruit, always shaded and cool. Winds blow gentle and fragrant with the sweetness of flowers.

Uttarakanda · Sarga 42 (Description of Rama Rajya)

Sarga 40–43 🌅

Rama Rajya — The Ideal Kingdom

Under Rama's just rule, Ayodhya becomes paradise on earth. No disease, no injustice, no sorrow — the eternal vision of righteous governance.

Golden Age
Sarga 44–46 🌑

Sita's Exile

Rama exiles the pregnant Sita to uphold his duty as king. It is the most heartbreaking act of dharma in the entire epic.

Tragic Duty
Sarga 93–96 🎵

Lava & Kusha Sing the Ramayana

Valmiki's students sing the entire Ramayana at Rama's court. Rama does not know the singers are his own sons.

Reunion
Sarga 97 🌍

Sita Returns to the Earth

Sita calls upon her mother the Earth. The ground opens in flowers. She descends — and is gone. The most transcendent farewell in all literature.

Transcendence
Sarga 108–111 🌊

Rama's Divine Return

Rama walks into the Sarayu river. The gods descend. The avatar completes his purpose and returns to his eternal form as Vishnu.

The Return
